Car Components Overview

Engine

The engine is the heart of a vehicle, converting fuel or electricity into mechanical power. It determines performance, acceleration, efficiency, and overall driving character. From inline engines to powerful V12s, engine design defines a car’s identity.

Turbocharger

A turbocharger increases engine power by forcing more compressed air into the combustion chamber. This results in improved horsepower and torque without significantly increasing engine size, making modern engines both powerful and efficient.

Transmission

The transmission system transfers power from the engine to the wheels. It adjusts gear ratios to balance speed and torque, ensuring smooth acceleration, fuel efficiency, and optimal performance under different driving conditions.

Wheel Drive System

The drive system controls how engine power is distributed to the wheels. Whether Front-Wheel Drive, Rear-Wheel Drive, or All-Wheel Drive, it significantly impacts handling, traction, and overall driving dynamics.

Suspension

The suspension system absorbs road imperfections and maintains tire contact with the road. It ensures stability, ride comfort, and precise handling, especially during high-speed driving and sharp cornering.

Braking System

The braking system slows down or stops a vehicle safely. Advanced systems like ABS and carbon-ceramic brakes improve stopping power, safety, and performance in both everyday driving and motorsport conditions.
